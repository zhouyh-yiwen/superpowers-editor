# Content Angle Style

This file defines how editorial brainstorming should sound and what it should optimize for.

## What Good Looks Like

- Sounds like an editor or strategist making a sharp judgment
- Starts from reader attention, not abstract admiration
- Ties every angle to a concrete reason someone would care
- Uses material from the book: a conflict, claim, surprise, scene, argument, author position, or contradiction
- Prefers clear language over decorative language

## Angle Checklist

Every viable angle should answer:

1. Who is this for?
2. Why would they stop and care?
3. What promise does this angle make?
4. What material from the book supports it?
5. Why is this angle better than the obvious alternatives?

When the source is a full book, also ask:

6. Which chapter, scene, example, or argument gives this angle texture?
7. Which details are concrete enough to be reused later in a post or recommendation piece?

## Borrowed Principles

From copywriting:
- Audience first
- Purpose first
- Hook matters
- Do not exaggerate
- Do not fake authority

From content creation:
- Match the output to its actual use
- If the goal is a post angle, do not drift into a full article draft
- If the goal is positioning, do not drift into a launch plan

From humanize:
- Avoid stiff AI phrasing
- Avoid empty transitions like "In today's fast-paced world"
- Avoid inflated claims like "revolutionary" unless the source material truly supports them
- Prefer natural sentence rhythm and direct wording

## Avoid

- "This book is thought-provoking and timely"
- "This content will resonate with a broad audience"
- "This angle is unique and compelling"
- any angle that could fit ten unrelated books

## Prefer

- angles that expose a tension
- angles that identify a reader problem
- angles that offer a concrete promise
- angles that can be defended with material already in hand
- angles that can point to reusable scenes, arguments, or details from the text
- angles that reveal what the real selling point is
- angles that help distinguish the book from nearby titles
- angles that reduce breadth and make the theme feel focused
- angles that suggest what kind of title or framing might actually attract attention
