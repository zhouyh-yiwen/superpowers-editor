# Editorial Report Style

Use this file when organizing an internal book proposal or approval report.

## Core Tone

- Sound like an editor making a recommendation to decision-makers.
- Be decisive without sounding inflated.
- Prefer concrete reasoning over admiration.
- Keep internal persuasion separate from reader-facing promotion.

## What Each Common Section Should Do

### Proposal Summary
- State what the book is, who it is for, and why it matters now.
- Short and high-signal.

### Reader And Scenario
- Name the readers in terms of needs, tensions, or situations.
- Avoid labels with no scenario attached.

### Publication Value
- Explain what the book offers: author credibility, quality of treatment, readability, theme strength, line fit.

### Publication Necessity
- Explain why this book deserves resources now.
- Ground it in at least one of:
  - present discussion or timing
  - knowledge gap
  - market gap
  - line development need

### Comparable Titles / Market Situation
- Show where this book sits, not just what else exists.
- Use comparison to clarify difference, not to pad the report.

### Marketing Possibility
- Mention likely handles, channels, or talking points.
- Do not drift into full campaign copy.

## Avoid

- repeating the content summary inside every section
- empty praise like "highly valuable" with no support
- vague reader labels with no reading scenario
- treating marketing possibility as pure optimism

## Prefer

- "This proposal works because..."
- "The strongest reason to publish now is..."
- "The most natural reader entry point is..."
- "Compared with existing titles, this book..."
